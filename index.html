The Foreigner’s Essential Guide

The Fideicomiso (Bank Trust)

Foreigners legally own residential land in the 'Restricted Zone' (near coasts) via a 50-year renewable bank trust, granting full ownership rights.

Closing Costs

Expect to budget 5% to 7% of the purchase price for acquisition taxes, notary fees, and trust setup costs.

Capital Gains Strategy

Retain all official tax receipts (facturas) for improvements; these are essential to offset capital gains tax when you sell.

The Role of the Notario

A Notario Público is a government-appointed lawyer who ensures the deed is valid and taxes are paid. They protect the transaction legality.

ROI Expectations

Playa del Carmen typically yields 8-12% annual ROI in high-demand zones like Playacar, driven by year-round tourism.

AMPI Certification

In Mexico, always verify your agent is AMPI certified. This ensures they follow a code of ethics and legal standards.

The Mayan Train

Infrastructure leads to appreciation. This multi-billion dollar project is currently driving up land values near station hubs.

Healthcare Access

The region hosts JCI-accredited hospitals like Costamed and Hospiten, offering world-class care at a fraction of US costs.

Residency vs. Tourist

Temporary residency makes opening local bank accounts and managing utilities significantly easier than being on a tourist visa.

Escrow Services

For maximum security, we utilize US-based third-party escrow services to hold funds until all closing conditions are met.
