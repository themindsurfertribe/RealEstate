Regulatory Briefing

Intelligence for Foreign Capital

Legal Sovereignty

Foreigners acquire 100% of property rights through the Fideicomiso—a state-sanctioned bank trust. This structure grants irrevocable ownership and the right to lease, sell, or bequeath the asset.

Capital Outlay Protocol

A provision of 5-7% of the total acquisition value is standard to cover the statutory taxes, high-level Notarial fees, and trust registration requirements.

Tax Mitigation Strategy

Maintaining a record of 'Facturas' (tax-compliant receipts) is the primary defensive strategy against future Capital Gains liabilities during asset liquidation.

The Notarial Protocol

In Mexico, the Notario Público is a high-ranking state official. Their intervention serves as the ultimate guarantee that the asset is transferred with zero encumbrances.

Yield Performance

Capital Custody

We mandate the utilization of US-based escrow accounts for all international transactions. Capital is only disbursed upon full Notarial verification of title.
